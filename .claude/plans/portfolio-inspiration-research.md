# Portfolio Design Inspiration Research

Curated list of innovative, visually stunning developer portfolios (2024-2025) that break the mold.

---

## 1. Dennis Snellenberg

- **URL:** https://dennissnellenberg.com
- **What makes it stand out:** The gold standard for freelance creative developer portfolios. Custom cursor with magnetic pull effect on interactive elements. Buttery-smooth Lenis scroll. Every hover state is meticulously crafted. Seamless page transitions with Barba.js. Typography-driven layout with massive type contrasts.
- **Key techniques:** Custom magnetic cursor, Lenis smooth scroll, GSAP animations, Barba.js page transitions, Framer Motion, Next.js, Styled Components, magnetic hover buttons
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Refined dark minimalism. Monochromatic palette with surgical precision in spacing and typography. Feels premium and effortless.

---

## 2. Stefan Vitasovic

- **URL:** https://stefanvitasovic.dev
- **What makes it stand out:** Swiss print design meets creative coding. Characters animate individually with glass-like parallax. LED overlay + noise grain on video textures create a consistent visual thread across all media. Infinite smooth scrolling on About page. WebGL shader effects with vertex displacement.
- **Key techniques:** Framer Motion character-by-character text animation, Three.js + React Three Fiber shaders, LED/noise grain overlays, SCSS modules, infinite smooth scroll with virtual scroll engine, Lethargy scroll detection, AnimatePresence page transitions
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Raw, experimental Swiss minimalism. Offset grids with generous whitespace. Angular geometric shapes. Unpolished-on-purpose feel that reads as intentional and confident.

---

## 3. Bruno Simon

- **URL:** https://bruno-simon.com
- **What makes it stand out:** The most iconic Three.js portfolio ever made. Visitors control a 3D vehicle to physically navigate through the portfolio — driving over ramps, through obstacles, and interacting with objects. Fully gamified experience that redefined what a portfolio could be.
- **Key techniques:** Three.js, Cannon.js physics engine, 3D environment navigation, real-time physics simulation, gamified UX
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Playful, toybox-like 3D world. Low-poly aesthetic with warm lighting. Feels like a video game, not a website.

---

## 4. Brittany Chiang

- **URL:** https://brittanychiang.com (current) / https://v4.brittanychiang.com (v4)
- **What makes it stand out:** The most cloned developer portfolio for good reason. Dark navy background with teal/green accent color. Cursor-following spotlight glow effect. Navigation highlights active section on scroll. "Doctor Who tardis" version history Easter egg. Proves that restraint and polish beat complexity.
- **Key techniques:** Gatsby (v4), sticky sidebar navigation, scroll-spy highlighting, cursor-following glow/spotlight, subtle hover animations, text-heavy minimal layout, green accent on dark background
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Dark, clean, developer-focused. Navy (#0a192f) with teal accent. Generous whitespace. Professional and approachable. The benchmark for "clean dev portfolio."

## 5. Samsy (Samuel Honigstein)

- **URL:** https://samsy.ninja
- **What makes it stand out:** A full cyberpunk 3D world built with WebGPU achieving 120+ FPS. First-person controls through a neon-lit cityscape. Holographic interfaces. Japanese design elements woven into the environment. Over 50 international awards.
- **Key techniques:** WebGPU (not just WebGL), first-person 3D navigation, holographic UI elements, neon lighting systems, particle effects, Japanese typography integration
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Cyberpunk neon noir. Dark with electric neon accents. Feels like walking through Blade Runner. Immersive and otherworldly.

## 6. Sebastien Lempens

- **URL:** https://sebastien-lempens.com
- **What makes it stand out:** A scroll-driven cinematic tour through 3D Paris. Camera perspective shifts between following the developer on a scooter and skydiving sequences. The scroll itself tells a story with dramatically shifting viewpoints.
- **Key techniques:** Three.js, scroll-driven 3D camera movement, perspective shifting (first-person to bird's eye), cinematic storytelling through scroll, environmental 3D modeling
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Cinematic, adventurous, Parisian. The portfolio feels like a short film you control with your scroll wheel.

## 7. Gregory Lalle

- **URL:** https://gregorylalle.com
- **What makes it stand out:** Awwwards Site of the Day. Deliberately stripped away WebGL and unnecessary animations to create something brutalist and original. Proves that restraint can be more impactful than technical excess. Clean, non-intrusive animations that serve the content.
- **Key techniques:** Brutalist layout, purposeful minimalist animation, clean typography, deliberate absence of WebGL, original grid layouts
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Brutalist minimalism. Anti-trend in the best way. Feels confident and mature. Design quality speaks through restraint rather than spectacle.

## 8. Jesse Zhou

- **URL:** https://www.jesse-zhou.com
- **What makes it stand out:** An entire 3D ramen hut that visitors navigate. Projects are displayed on a vending machine. Video work plays on a television within the scene. Completely unique metaphor — the portfolio IS a place.
- **Key techniques:** Three.js, 3D spatial navigation, object interaction (vending machine, TV), environmental storytelling, themed 3D world-building
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Whimsical, warm, Japanese-inspired. Cozy and inviting. A portfolio you want to explore because the space itself is delightful.

## 9. Olha Lazarieva & Max Milkin (Two portfolios, one process)

- **Olha URL:** https://olhalazarieva.com
- **Max URL:** https://maxmilkin.com
- **What makes them stand out:** Two complementary portfolios built collaboratively. Olha's uses mouse-reactive 3D camera orbiting, baked lighting, and GSAP ScrollTrigger controlling room rotations. Max's features a PixiJS WebGL animated loader with rotating text rings and GSAP MotionPathPlugin orchestrating scattered letters that assemble into paragraphs.
- **Key techniques:** GSAP (ScrollTrigger, MotionPathPlugin, timelines), Three.js + React Three Fiber, PixiJS WebGL, Blender, baked texture lighting, letter choreography animations, curved motion paths, parallax effects
- **Aesthetic/mood:** Minimal, controlled precision. Black-and-white with cinematic transitions (Olha). Technical depth with intricate letter choreography (Max). Both prioritize calm, intentional motion over flashy effects.

## 10. Jordan Cruz-Correa

- **URL:** https://jccdev.vercel.app
- **What makes it stand out:** An entire Windows 98 desktop environment as a portfolio. Interactive Notepad app, functional Recycle Bin with themed files, playable audio. Nostalgia-driven UI that's fully functional and coherent. Shows personality and attention to detail.

## Design Techniques Catalog (for our portfolio)

### Scroll & Animation
- **Lenis** — Native browser smooth scrolling (no CSS transform hijacking)
- **GSAP ScrollTrigger** — Scroll-based animation triggers
- **Framer Motion / Motion** — React-native declarative animations, AnimatePresence for page transitions
- **Character-by-character text reveals** — Split text into individual chars, stagger animation
- **Parallax depth layers** — Elements moving at different scroll speeds

### Cursor & Interaction
- **Custom cursor** — Replace default with branded circle/dot that scales on hover
- **Magnetic buttons** — Elements that "pull" toward cursor on proximity (GSAP)
- **Cursor spotlight/glow** — Radial gradient following mouse position (Brittany Chiang style)
- **Cursor trail** — Multiple elements following with elastic easing delays

### Visual Texture
- **Noise/grain overlay** — CSS or SVG filter grain texture on backgrounds/images
- **LED overlay on media** — Scanline + noise effect on video/images
- **Glassmorphism** — Frosted glass blur effects on cards
- **Gradient mesh backgrounds** — Animated multi-color gradient blobs

### Typography
- **Massive type contrasts** — 8-12rem headings vs 1rem body
- **Variable font animations** — Animating font-weight/width on scroll/hover
- **Split text animations** — Words/characters animating in individually
- **Monospace for accents** — Mixing a monospace font for labels/tags with a display font for headings

### Layout
- **Bento grid** — Modular rectangular sections inspired by Japanese bento boxes
- **Offset/asymmetric grids** — Breaking the traditional column grid
- **Marquee/ticker** — Infinite horizontal scrolling text bands
- **Horizontal scroll sections** — Sections that scroll horizontally within vertical scroll

### Navigation
- **Scroll-spy active indicators** — Highlight current section in nav
- **Sticky sidebar nav** — Fixed navigation that tracks scroll position
- **Full-screen menu overlays** — Dramatic menu reveal animations
- **Progress indicators** — Scroll progress bar or section dots
